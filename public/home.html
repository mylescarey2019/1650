Here at 1650 learn about starting an Individual Retirement Account

As a 16 or 17 year old with a part time job you can invest in your account with the help of your parents

  • In this example saving $10 a month in an IRA starting at age 36 could grow to $15,000 (•••) by age 65

  • Starting at age 26 it could grow to $35,000 (– – –) by 65

  • But if started at age 16 it could grow to $80,000 (─)

Students

You probably haven't given retirement any thought
Starting now lets Time and Compound Interest work for you

  • If you have a part time job you can start now

  • You don't need a lot to start investing - you could start with $10 or $20 a month

  • Since the 1920s the average yearly Stock Market return rate is around 10%

  • That means $10 invested today could grow to $500 over the next 50 years

  • $20 a month for the next 50 years could grow to $160,000 or more

  • See the Resource Links to learn more about personal finance for teens

  • Talk to your parents about starting a Custodial IRA

Parents

Most parents hope their children more success then they have had
Starting a Custodial IRA for your child can help

  • Consider your own retirement savings - where might it be if you had started at age 16 or 17 ?

  • Starting an account is not difficult, some finanical institutions open accounts with no minimum deposit

  • Time is Money - consider this scenario: John starts investing $20,000 a year at age 40.   Jane starts investing $5,000 a year at age 21.   Assume a 10% return rate.   By age 65 Jane will have almost twice as much as John even though she invested about half as much

  • If your child has a part time job consider helping them get started on a retirement nest egg

Custodial Individual Retirement Accounts

Custodial IRAs allow minors with earned income to get a jump start on investing

  • Custodian/parent has control of the account until the minor is 18 ( 21 in some states)

  • Account can be used for college expenses or retirement

  • Contributions come from the minor's job income or self-employment income such as babysitting

  • Contributions cannot be more than the minor's yearly earned income, but the minor or parent/grandparent can contribute

  • See the Resource Links for more information including how to open an account
