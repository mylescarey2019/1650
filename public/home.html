Here at 1650 learn about starting an Individual Retirement Account

As a 16 or 17 year old with a part time job you can invest in your account with the help of your parents

  • In this example saving $10 a month in an IRA starting at age 36 could grow to $15,000 after 50 years

  • Starting at age 26 it could grow to $35,000 after 50 years

  • But if started at age 16 it could grow to $80,000

  • That is Time & Compound Interest working for you

Custodial Individual Retirement Accounts

Custodial IRAs allow minors with earned income to get a jump start on investing

  • The Custodian (usually a parent) has control of the account until the minor turns 18 ( or 21 in some states)

  • The account can be used for college expenses but has the best chance of building a sizable nest egg if held for the long term retirement letting compound interest work

  • Contributions can come from the minor's formal earned income or self-employment income such as babysitting

  • Contributions cannot be more than the minor's yearly earned income, but they can come from the minor's earnings and/or contributions from a parent or grandparent

  • See the Resource Links for more information including how to open and account
